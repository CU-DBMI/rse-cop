Use this checklist when planning, running, and closing out a Research Software Engineering Community of Practice meeting.

## Prep

- [ ] Pick a date, recognizing that Wednesdays often have the most in-person attendance on campus.
- [ ] Select a "next time" date so attendees can hear when the following meeting is expected to happen.
- [ ] Reserve a conference space, such as AHSB 6242 or the 7th floor conference space, through the [EMS system](https://cu.emscloudservice.com/web/).
- [ ] Create a calendar invite, referencing previous events for content guidance. Invite the internal RSE-CoP calendar group for attendees, and forward to others as needed.
- [ ] Create slides, optionally referencing [previous slides](https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1w6Nq-YVR0yqLwwLfZhQPqc1hiY9q6bIr).
- [ ] Create a GitHub issue for the meeting agenda using the meeting agenda issue template, referencing [previous meeting agenda issues](https://github.com/CU-DBMI/rse-cop/issues) for content guidance.
- [ ] Invite lightning talks if desired or as needed.

## During the meeting

- [ ] Go to the room early to ensure the technology will work for remote attendees and presentation slides.
- [ ] Join the room with the Zoom meeting.
- [ ] Ensure other attendees can share the slides via Zoom or the in-room connection.
- [ ] Seek input and engagement from the audience.
- [ ] Communicate when the next meeting will be.
- [ ] Clean up the space afterwards, including exiting the Zoom meeting and leaving the room tidy.

## After the meeting

- [ ] Share the agenda and resources via the [past meetings page](/rse-cop/past-meetings/) on this repo.
- [ ] Close out the GitHub issue for the meeting.
- [ ] Pass this page and any other related guidance to the next lead for the next meeting.
